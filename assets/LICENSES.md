## Air and naval sprites ‚Äî provenance to confirm

The isometric aircraft and ship sprites (8 units √ó 3 team rows, 64√ó64 RGBA) were
supplied by the project owner as a `Grid Commander Design System` handoff and
vendored verbatim under `sprites/iso_air_sea/`. They replaced the generated
placeholders that previously occupied those columns.

**Their origin and license are not recorded here yet, and this table is the
project's rule for third-party assets.** If they are original or commissioned
work, say so and mark this row accordingly; if they came from a pack, name it
and its license. Until that is filled in, treat this as an open item ‚Äî not as a
clearance.

Rebuild the atlas from them with `make tiles`.

## Commander portraits ‚Äî placeholder status

The portraits under `portraits/` are generated, project-original placeholders,
not the final art. The commander-readiness plan (`.lavish/commander-readiness-plan.html`,
decision D1) makes the supplied Claude card sheet the production source *after* a
reuse-rights check; until that art pass lands, these procedural busts keep the
card, selection, and battle-identity work unblocked. They contain no third-party
pixels ‚Äî each is drawn from the commander's faction colour and the mechanic-led
visual cue in the plan's roster table. Rebuild with `make portraits`.

## PixVoxel Revised Wargame Sprites

- **Author:** Tommy Ettinger
- **License:** CC0 1.0 ‚Äî public domain dedication, no attribution required (we
  credit it anyway)
- **Source:** <https://opengameart.org/content/pixvoxel-revised-isometric-wargame-sprites>
- **Archive:** `Revised_PixVoxel_Wargame_1.7z` ‚Üí `Revised_PixVoxel_Wargame/standing_frames/`

The pack is isometric-only, so its *ground* tiles (isometric elevation slabs)
are unusable on this square top-down grid and are not vendored. Only sprites
that sit **on** a tile ‚Äî units and property buildings ‚Äî are used, and they drop
into the existing grid unchanged.

Every sprite is the `Large`, `face0`, first standing frame, so the whole roster
shares one facing. Team rows come from the pack's palettes: **red = `color2`**,
**blue = `color6`**. Neutral is **`color1` desaturated to grey** ‚Äî `color0`
(dark) is the pack's nominal neutral but its red trim reads as the red team.

### Vendored sources

The 36 source PNGs the atlases are built from ‚Äî the 3 palettes above √ó the 9
units and 3 buildings mapped below, each `<palette>_<sprite>_Large_face0_0.png`
‚Äî are vendored under `sprites/pixvoxel_src/` (62 KB). CC0 permits this, and it
makes `make tiles` reproducible on a fresh clone with no download. The rest of
the pack is deliberately not vendored. A `.gdignore` there keeps Godot from
importing them as game assets; they are build inputs, not runtime resources.

Rebuild with `make tiles`, which uses those by default. To build from a full
extracted pack instead, override `PIXVOXEL`; see
`tools/build_pixvoxel_atlases.sh`.

### Unit mapping

The PixVoxel roster is smaller than ours and has no bazooka/scout/light-vs-heavy
tank/AA distinctions by name, so several entries are stand-ins chosen for a
unique silhouette. `_P`/`_S`/`_T` are the pack's weapon variants.

| Our unit | `atlas_col` | PixVoxel sprite | Match |
|---|---|---|---|
| infantry | 0 | `Infantry` | direct ‚Äî rifleman |
| mech | 1 | `Infantry_T` | direct ‚Äî the launcher-armed footsoldier |
| recon | 2 | `Supply_T` | **stand-in** ‚Äî radar van; no scout/bike in the pack, picked for the mast silhouette |
| tank | 3 | `Tank` | direct ‚Äî treads, single cannon |
| md_tank | 4 | `Tank_P` | **stand-in** ‚Äî no light/heavy split; the bulkier twin-barrel variant |
| anti_air | 5 | `Artillery_S` | **stand-in** ‚Äî no dedicated AA vehicle; twin tubes angled skyward |
| artillery | 6 | `Artillery` | direct ‚Äî raised howitzer barrel |
| rockets | 7 | `Artillery_T` | direct ‚Äî missile cluster |
| apc | 8 | `Supply` | direct ‚Äî plain box van |

The pack stops there: it has no aircraft and no ships, so every unit at
`atlas_col` 9 or higher carries no PixVoxel pixels at all. Those columns are
project-original placeholder silhouettes drawn by
`tools/generate_unit_placeholders.gd` (`make unit-placeholders`), which leaves
the nine columns mapped above byte-identical.
