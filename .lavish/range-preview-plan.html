Grid Commander · Stretch item · Proposed for review · rev 1

Range Preview — enemy intel and the fire ring

Today, clicking an enemy unit does nothing — the one question a tactics player asks all game long ("how far can that thing reach?") is answered by counting tiles on the wallpaper. This plan adds two moves of the same idea. Click any unit you cannot command — an enemy, or one of your own that has already acted — and its movement range paints in the familiar blue. Press R while any move range is on screen — yours or theirs — and the cells that unit could bring under fire this turn paint in red. Both are pure reads: no command is issued, nothing in the sim moves, and every cell shown is computed by the same single authorities the game already trusts to resolve the real thing.

Blue — where it can move Red — where it can fire R — one key, one meaning Fog-honest by the same gate as targeting
1

The seams already exist

Almost nothing here is new machinery. The overlays, the math, the fog gate and even the free key are all sitting in the codebase waiting to be introduced to each other:

Both overlays exist, already in the right colors

battle_view.gd:275 paint_move_overlay paints the blue TileMapLayer (modulate at battle.tscn:21) and battle_view.gd:282 paint_attack_overlay the red one (battle.tscn:24). Both take a cell list, both clear on an empty array. The features are new callers of a painting API that ships today — no new nodes, no new tiles.

The AI already computes the exact answer

ai/threat_map.gd:55 _firing_cells + _mark_ring is precisely "every cell this unit could bring under fire this turn": indirect units shoot only from where they stand (AttackRange.is_indirect, attack_range.gd:62), direct units from every cell they can stop on, ring clamped to [minimum, maximum]. Difficult-tier AI has trusted this math for a while. The plan promotes the geometry to the authority that owns "who and how far" — core/rules/attack_range.gd — and has both the AI and the UI ask it (D1).

The entry point is a no-op today

In State.IDLE, confirm_at (battle.gd:288-295) selects only team == current_team and not acted; an enemy unit, or your own waited unit, falls through every branch and the click evaporates. The preview slots into exactly that dead branch — it converts no-ops into information and changes no click that does something today.

The fog gate and the key are ready

battle_view.gd:331 can_see_unit is the gate targeting already uses — battle.gd:706: "the player cannot target what they cannot see." The preview takes the same gate, so a fogged or dived unit is exactly as unclickable as it is untargetable. And the input map (project.godot:30-77) leaves R entirely unbound — R for range costs nothing.

2

How it plays

One grammar, whoever owns the unit: blue is movement, red is fire, R flips the red on and off. The boards below are open ground for readability — the real overlay is the same flood fill the sim uses, so terrain, fuel and blockers shape it exactly as they shape a real move.

① Click the enemy tank

Its move range paints in the same blue yours does. No command is issued — the sim never hears about it.

② Press R — the fire ring

A direct unit can fire from any cell it can stop on, so its ring is the move area grown by its range: everything red can be hit this turn. Red paints over blue; one more press of R and you are back to blue.

③ The artillery lesson

An indirect unit cannot move and fire, so its red ring stands around its current cell — and the min-range hole is finally visible: stand next to the gun and it cannot touch you. This one picture is the feature's best argument.

move overlay (blue) fire overlay (red) enemy unit open ground (mock)

The interaction grammar

  • Click an enemy unit, or your own waited unit → its move range paints blue and the game enters a new PREVIEW state. The terrain panel keeps narrating the hovered cell as it does now.
  • Press R while any move range is on screen — your selected unit in UNIT_SELECTED, or a previewed unit — → that unit's fire cells paint red on the attack overlay. Press again → red clears, blue remains.
  • Click another unit while previewing → the preview switches; clicking one of your ready units leaves the preview and selects it normally — intel never blocks play.
  • Click empty ground or press cancel → the preview dismisses back to IDLE. Deliberately not the map menu: a click that clears information should not also open something.
  • Every exit clears the red — confirming a move, opening a menu, cancelling, switching units. The ring is a momentary lens, never a stale layer.
  • Unarmed units (transports, the lander) paint no red — same truth the terrain panel tells by omitting their "Rng" line.
3

Decisions

All five are proposed, not locked — annotate the ones you'd call differently. D3's toggle-versus-hold and D2's red-over-blue are the two most taste-shaped calls.

D1 · Where is the ring computed? One authority, promoted proposed

A new static on the class that already owns "who and how far": AttackRange.threat_cells(state, unit) → Array[Vector2i] — the deduped union of [minimum, maximum] rings around every cell the unit could fire from this turn (its own cell for an indirect unit, per attack_range.gd:62; every can_stop_at cell of MovementResolver.reachable for a direct one). ai/threat_map.gd then refactors to ask it — build() keeps its own policy filter and its per-enemy cell attribution, but the geometry lives in exactly one place. This is the movement-overlay lesson applied before the bug instead of after: the repo already paid once for a second opinion on movement (CLAUDE.md, the MoveCommand.validate story), and a red overlay that disagreed with the AI's threat model by one cell would be the same bug wearing a new color. Rejected: computing the ring in battle.gd beside _attackable_cells — that would make the presentation layer the second opinion.

D2 · One grammar of colors — blue moves, red fires, red wins on top proposed

The enemy's move range paints the same blue as yours, and the fire ring paints the red attack overlay above it (the layer order battle.tscn already draws). The colors mean the question, not the owner: blue answers "where can it stand", red answers "where can it hit", and the clicked unit plus the terrain panel say whose answer it is. Rejected: tinting the enemy preview in its faction color via SideIdentity — pretty on paper, but a Meridian-red army's move range would sit one hue away from the fire red, and an overlay a player must disambiguate by faction is worse than one they never have to think about. For a direct unit the red covers the blue entirely (the ring is a superset of the move area) — that is the honest picture, it is one keypress to flip back, and if playtest wants both at once, painting only the fringe is a one-line variant parked for that conversation.

D3 · The key and the verb — R, as a toggle proposed

A new input action show_range bound to R (unbound today; project.godot edited minimally, per house rules), handled where all board input lives — _unhandled_input, event-driven like every other action in the file. Toggle, not hold: press to show, press to clear, and any state transition clears it for you. A mouse player planning a flank wants the ring to stay up while the cursor is busy elsewhere; hold-to-peek fights the hand that is already on the mouse. The alternative — hold to peek, release to restore blue — is cheap to build on the same seam if the toggle feels sticky in play. The action works in exactly the two states that show a move range (UNIT_SELECTED and the new PREVIEW) and is inert everywhere else.

D4 · Dry guns still show their ring — capability, not forecast proposed

A unit out of ammo paints its red ring all the same. The overlay answers "what does this weapon reach", the same capability the terrain panel's Rng 2-3 line states regardless of the ammo counter beside it — and a rocket truck one resupply away from full is not a safe neighbor. The AI's ThreatMap.build keeps its dry-and-unarmed filter (threat_map.gd:43): it measures expected damage this turn, a genuinely different question, and both callers keep asking the same geometry underneath. Truly unarmed units (max_range ≤ 0) return an empty ring from the authority itself — nothing to show, nothing shown.

D5 · The preview reads the real state, gated by the viewer's eyes proposed

Entry is gated by view.can_see_unit — the exact gate targeting uses (battle.gd:706), so under fog a hidden unit is unclickable and a dived submarine reveals nothing by being probed. Computation then runs against the real GameState through the single authorities, not against a fog-redacted copy: building a second, censored board for the flood fill to walk would be one more independent opinion on movement, and that is the one bug this repo has promised itself never to buy twice. The cost is a leak so small it needs a magnifying glass — see R3 — and the sim stays untouched either way: forecasting is a pure read, and so is this.

4

Milestones — each one shippable

RP1 · The geometry authority size S · core only

Promote the ring math into core/rules/attack_range.gd and make the AI its first caller — zero pixels change, and the class that owns "who and how far" finally owns "from where, after moving" too.

  • AttackRange.threat_cells(state, unit) → Array[Vector2i] — firing cells (indirect: standing cell; direct: every can_stop_at cell) dilated by the [minimum, maximum] ring, deduped, clipped to the board. Pure read, no RNG, no mutation.
  • ai/threat_map.gd refactored: build() keeps its dry/unarmed policy filter and per-enemy attribution, the geometry comes from the authority. incoming_damage untouched.
  • tests/unit/test_attack_range.gd — the authority's first dedicated test file: direct dilation on open ground, indirect ring pinned to the standing cell, the min-range hole, unarmed → empty, a friendly blocker deleting a stoppable cell and its fringe, board-edge clipping.
  • Proof: make test green, and — the balance plan's merge bar, honored — fixed-seed make commander-balance and make difficulty-check reports byte-identical before and after. A diff means the geometry changed, not merely moved.

RP2 · The fire ring on your own unit size S

The R key, wired for the selection flow that already exists — useful the day it lands, before any enemy preview.

  • show_range action in project.godot, bound to R — a minimal, reviewable hand edit in the house style of confirm/cancel.
  • battle.gd: in UNIT_SELECTED, show_range toggles view.paint_attack_overlay(AttackRange.threat_cells(game, selected)); a _range_shown flag, nothing more. Path planning under the cursor keeps working beneath it.
  • Every exit clears it: _clear_selection already blanks the attack overlay (battle.gd:344); _animate_move (battle.gd:351-354) learns to — today it clears only the move overlay and path line. The state machine keeps the ring and TARGETING's target cells from ever sharing the layer.
  • Indirect units read honestly: ring around the standing cell while blue shows the move range — "move or fire" in one picture.
  • Proof: played — tank, artillery, a transport (no ring); make screenshot byte-identical (no capture path presses R); make test green untouched.

RP3 · The preview — click what you cannot command size M

The dead click comes alive: enemies and waited units answer with their move range, and R works there too.

  • New State.PREVIEW in the battle.gd enum, entered from the IDLE branch: elif unit != null and view.can_see_unit(unit): _enter_preview(unit) — slotted after the select branch, before the factory/menu fallthroughs, converting only clicks that do nothing today.
  • _enter_preview: select tick, dedicated _previewed / _preview_range fields — selected keeps meaning "a unit I am commanding", an invariant the menu flow leans on — MovementResolver.reachable, blue overlay. No path line: a preview plans nothing.
  • In PREVIEW: show_range toggles the red ring off _previewed; clicking another visible unit switches the preview or selects your ready unit through the normal path; empty click and cancel dismiss to IDLE; cursor motion keeps feeding the terrain panel.
  • The input-block lists at battle.gd:244/254 stay as they are — PREVIEW accepts board input like IDLE; the scenario driver waits on states it never enters and needs nothing.
  • Proof: played with fog on — a visible enemy previews, a hidden one stays a dead click, a dived sub reveals nothing; every dismissal path exercised; make screenshot byte-identical; suite green.
5

Risks

R1 · The refactor moves the AI's aim

RP1 rebuilds ThreatMap on the promoted authority; a one-cell drift in the geometry would silently retune the Difficult tier. Mitigation is the gate the balance plan already built for exactly this shape of change: both fixed-seed reports byte-identical, or the milestone does not merge. Two committed documents rest on those numbers; they are the tripwire.

R2 · Overlay double-booking

The blue layer moonlights for drop-targeting and the red one for TARGETING's live target cells; a stale fire ring surviving into either would show a player cells that mean something else. Mitigation: the ring exists only inside the two range states, and every exit from them runs a named clear site (RP2's list). The audit is a grep for paint_attack_overlay — a handful of call sites, all in one file.

R3 · Red-over-blue swallows the move range for direct units

Press R on a tank and the whole picture turns red — correct (everything it can reach, it can hit) but a player who wanted both facts at once must toggle. If playtest finds that annoying, the fringe-only variant (paint red just where blue is not) is a one-line change at the single call site, and D2 has already argued both sides so the conversation is short.

R4 · Fog leakage by absence

D5 computes against the real board, so a hidden unit standing on a cell the previewed enemy could otherwise stop on trims a firing position, and a sharp-eyed player could read the missing fringe cell as "something is there." Bounded: absence-only, one ring cell at a time, and only where the viewer already suspects. The redacted-state alternative buys back this sliver at the price of a second movement model — D5 declines the trade on purpose.

R5 · A new state in a ten-state machine

Low: PREVIEW is behaviorally IDLE with two overlays and a dismissal grammar — it issues no commands, so no transition out of it can strand the sim. The scenario driver waits on named states and never enters this one; the play-proof in RP3 walks every dismissal path by hand.

6

Explicitly out of scope

The full danger map

"Show every enemy's range at once" — the genre's other classic. RP1's authority makes it a fold over visible enemies whenever we want it, but it is its own UX problem (a board painted wall-to-wall red is not information) and its own plan.

Damage numbers in the preview

"How hard would it hit me?" — forecast_at answers it today and ThreatMap.incoming_damage already sums it for the AI. A per-cell damage readout is a different surface (numbers, not cells) and stays parked.

Predicting intent

The preview shows what a unit could do, never what the AI will do. No path lines for enemy units, no "likely target" markers — capability is knowledge, intent is cheating.

Rebinding and controller support

R joins the fixed map beside Z and X. A key-rebinding screen or a gamepad chord for show_range is an input-settings feature for the day the input map grows one.
