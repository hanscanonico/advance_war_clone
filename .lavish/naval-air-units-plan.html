Air Naval Milestones N1–N4

Naval & Air Units

Nine new units across two new movement domains, five new terrains, and the fuel-upkeep rule that keeps planes honest — built almost entirely as data on top of systems the APC, the damage chart, and the movement resolver already ship.

All four milestones shipped. Decisions D1–D6 in §11 held; §13 records where the build diverged from this plan and why.

1 · Why this is cheaper than it looks

The M0–M7 base game was built so that a unit is a .tres file and a matchup is a dictionary entry. Nearly every mechanic air and naval units need already exists and is already tested — what's genuinely new is small and listed on the right.

Already shipped & tested

  • Movement domains are data. A move class is a key in each terrain's move_costs; a missing key means impassable (core/terrain_type.gd:21). Sea is impassable today only because no class has a key there. MovementResolver needs zero changes.
  • Targeting restrictions are data. A missing damage-chart entry already means "cannot attack" (core/damage_chart.gd:14) — "infantry can't shoot a bomber" is an omission, not code.
  • Fuel & ammo per unit, spent on movement / per shot, refilled on properties and by suppliers (tests/unit/test_fuel_and_ammo.gd).
  • Transports: load / drop / supply commands, cargo riding along, cargo dying with its carrier, and the full battle-scene UI for all of it (APC).
  • Indirect fire (min/max range, no counters) — the battleship is a wet Rockets.
  • Charge, doctrines, saves all key off unit ids and exported id lists, so new units flow through commanders and the save codec untouched.

Genuinely new

  • Three move classes (air, ship, lander) and five terrains (airport, port, shoal, bridge, reef).
  • Facilities: which terrain builds / services which units — today &"base" is hardcoded in BuildCommand, the build menu, and the AI (§6).
  • Fuel upkeep & crash: air and sea units burn fuel every day and are destroyed at zero — the rule that makes a 22 000 bomber a commitment, not a fire-and-forget.
  • Per-transport cargo rules: the lander carries vehicles, the T-Copter doesn't — the global "foot/boot only" constant becomes per-type data.
  • Air ignores terrain stars — one gate in the damage formula.
  • Sub dive (N3) — the only feature that touches vision, targeting and the save format at once, so it gets its own milestone and its own go/no-go decision.
  • AI: build from facilities, respect fuel, and know what it cannot use (§7 — the honest risk of this whole plan).
Backwards-compatibility invariant: no existing map gains an airport or a port, so on every current map (and every current save) nothing new can be built and nothing changes — the whole feature is opt-in per map. The existing maps stay byte-identical.
Superseded 2026-07-21 — the control group did its job. The invariant above was a shipping constraint for the duration of N1–N4, not a permanent rule: while fuel, hulls and the facility-aware AI were unproven, the untouched boards were what proved the feature was additive. All four milestones are merged and the soaks are green, so isthmus, ironworks and crossfire have now exercised the "opt-in per map" this clause was written to allow. It is replaced by a conversion rule — cells may change class, land stays passable to every land class, sea is never carved, coastlines are never redrawn — which keeps saves (stored by map_path and reloaded from res://) safe across the edit. Audit, verdicts and per-map reasoning: .lavish/map-retrofit-plan.html.

New columns on existing terrain

  • air: 1 on every terrain — planes fly over mountains, rivers and the sea, and pay 1 fuel per tile. That single data rule is the whole air movement model.
  • ship: 1, lander: 1 on sea only (plus port and reef above). River stays land-locked; land terrain stays keyless for sea classes.
  • Enemy units still block paths for everyone, air included — same as Advance Wars, and already what the resolver does.

Two small TerrainType fields

  • builds: Array[StringName] — move classes this terrain can produce. Base = the four land classes; port = ship+lander; airport = air. Kills the &"base" hardcode in three places (§6).
  • services: Array[StringName] — domains this property repairs/resupplies. City/Base/HQ = land; port = sea; airport = air. A bomber parked on a city gets nothing — that's what makes airports matter.
  • conceals: bool — woods and reef; replaces the hardcoded woods id in core/rules/vision.gd:96.

5 · Fuel upkeep, crashing & the day cycle

The rule that separates air/naval from "faster tanks": planes and ships burn fuel just by existing, and are destroyed when the tank hits zero. Two new UnitType fields carry it: domain (land / air / sea) and fuel_upkeep. Land units have upkeep 0 and never crash — current behavior, untouched.

Per-unit order inside TurnRules.begin_turn

  1. Ready the unit (acted = false) — unchanged.
  2. Upkeep: fuel −= fuel_upkeep, floored at 0.
  3. Resupply if on a friendly property whose services include the unit's domain, or in reach of a supplier — the existing rule, now domain-gated. APC reach refuels anything, planes included (Advance Wars rule, and it makes Gideon Holt's extended supply radius suddenly very interesting).
  4. Crash check: an air or sea unit at 0 fuel is destroyed via remove_unit — cargo goes down with it, rout applies, and no charge is banked on either meter (self-inflicted losses feed no power).
  5. Repair (+2 HP, paid) — now behind the same services gate: airports repair planes, cities don't.

Order matters: upkeep before resupply means a bomber that lands on an airport is always safe; resupply before the crash check means it can never die on friendly tarmac. A fighter loitering in enemy airspace at 99 fuel has ~7 full-move days before the math catches it.

  • Movement fuel is untouched: 1 per tile for air (all terrain costs 1), per-terrain for ships — already billed by GameState.advance_unit.
  • Move budget is already fuel-capped (movement_resolver.gd:59), so a dry plane visibly shrinks on the range overlay before it ever crashes.
  • UI: the terrain panel already shows Fuel n/max; N1 adds a low-fuel warning badge on the sprite when fuel < upkeep + move_points (one turn of margin).

7 · The AI — scoped honestly

Movement, attack scoring and target selection are already generic: the planner reads the damage chart and the movement matrix, so a b-copter raid or a battleship bombardment falls out of existing code. Production and logistics do not. Three tiers, committed to explicitly:

In scope — N1/N2

  • Facility-aware building: _plan_build iterates every owned empty producing property; build_priority stays one data list, walked filtered by the facility's buildable classes. Infantry-first capture logic gates to land facilities.
  • Profile data gains air/naval entries (e.g. b_copter after tank, cruiser answer when enemy fields subs/bombers is N4 tuning, not code).
  • Fuel retreat: air/sea units below the one-turn fuel margin route to the nearest servicing property — extends the existing damaged-retreat goal in _advance_goal.

In scope — N3/N4

  • Sub policy, minimal: dive when an enemy who can hit a surfaced sub is in range and no cruiser is; surface to attack. The AI already respects hidden units via Vision.is_hidden_from — dived subs plug into that same single exception.
  • N4: tune build priorities per profile against the balance runner; make sure Anti-Air / Missiles appear once the opponent flies.

Explicitly out

  • Transport ferry planning (lander convoys, t-copter drops). Multi-turn load→sail→unload plans don't fit a greedy one-command planner; faking it badly reads as a broken AI. The AI therefore never builds a unit it cannot use: transports are skipped in its priorities.
  • Consequence for map design: AI-facing sea maps need direct-engagement lanes (bridges, shared coastlines, port-to-port gun range). Codified as R1.

9 · Milestones — each ends playable

N1 · Wings air + the foundations shipped

Air forces the general machinery through (facilities, services, upkeep/crash, cargo classes) with a visible payoff, while needing only one new terrain.

  • Core: air class · airport · builds/services · upkeep & crash · air-ignores-stars · cargo_classes (t-copter carries like an APC that flies).
  • Data: fighter, bomber, b-copter, t-copter, missiles; chart rows + lint test; air: 1 on all terrains.
  • AI: facility-aware build + fuel retreat. Map: "Jet Stream" — the first map with airports. UI: filtered build menu, low-fuel badge.
  • Done means: a full hotseat and vs-AI match on Jet Stream where air power decides games and runs dry when overextended; make verify green; old maps provably unchanged.

N2 · Tides the navy shipped

  • Core: ship/lander classes · port, shoal, bridge, reef · conceals generalization · lander unload gating.
  • Data: battleship, cruiser, sub (surfaced — no dive yet), lander; chart rows; sea gains ship/lander costs.
  • Map: "The Straits" — ports, shoals, a bridge chokepoint, sea lanes putting port-to-port gun range in play (AI-viable per R1).
  • Done means: naval battles work end-to-end incl. a human ferrying tanks by lander; AI builds and fights with capital ships on The Straits.

N3 · Depths sub dive — go/no-go is D5 shipped

  • Dive/Surface commands · Unit.dived · upkeep 5 while dived · targetable only by cruiser/sub · hidden except adjacent (fog and clear weather, like AW) · AI via the existing is_hidden_from exception · save v3 (additive) · minimal AI dive policy.
  • Done means: a dived sub ambushes in fog against human and AI; save/load round-trips it; soak stays clean.

N4 · Trim the fleet balance & doctrine pass shipped

  • Mixed-domain scenarios in the balance runner; chart/cost tuning from its CSVs.
  • Commander pass: extend exported id lists where doctrine says so (Cassian's heavy_ids and capital ships? Orin's scouts and the t-copter?) — each a one-line .tres edit, decided from runner data, not vibes.
  • Mixed-domain AI-vs-AI soak test joins the suite (the hook-layers integration net); README/CLAUDE.md document the new classes, symbols and rules.
  • Done means: no commander pairing swings past the base-game tolerance on mixed maps; docs current; suite green.

11 · Decisions — proposed, for review

D1 · Roster: nine units

4 air + 4 sea + Missiles. Missiles is included so ground players have a second answer to air. Alternative: minimal six (cut sub, missiles, fighter) — cheaper but the air game degenerates to bomber-rush-vs-AA.

D2 · Fuel upkeep + crash: yes

Per-day drain and destruction at zero for air/sea, in the §5 order; crashes bank no charge. Alternative — no upkeep — makes air strictly-better tanks and airports cosmetic.

D3 · Facilities as data

Port/airport are ordinary income properties with builds and services lists on TerrainType; cities no longer service planes. One mechanism replaces three hardcodes (BuildCommand, build menu, AI).

D4 · Transport matrix per type

Lander: capacity 2, any land class, unloads on shoal/port only. T-Copter: capacity 1, foot/boot, unloads anywhere the cargo can stand. Cruiser carries nothing (AW2 copter-cargo is out).

D5 · Sub dive ships as N3

Dive is the one mechanic touching vision + targeting + saves, so it is isolated where it can be cut without unwinding the navy. Fallback if cut: the sub stays a surfaced torpedo boat (still useful), N3 collapses into N4.

D6 · Air defense rules

Air defenders take 0 terrain stars (commander star bonuses still apply — the formula stays normative). Woods/reef concealment stays cell-based, so a copter over woods hides like a tank in them — a simplification we keep and document.

12 · Out of scope — named so it stays out

  • Carriers, stealth planes, black boats, battleship-carriers — AW2+/DS roster.
  • Cruisers carrying copters.
  • AI transport-ferry planning (named stretch goal; see R1).
  • Weather, per-map unit bans / category toggles.
  • Primary/secondary weapon split (one chart row per unit stands).
  • Naval terrain beyond §3 (no pipes, no missile silos).
  • New commanders themed on air/sea — doctrine extensions to existing generals happen in N4, new generals belong to the commanders plan.

13 · What shipped, and what the build taught

All four milestones landed with decisions D1–D6 intact. Three things the plan did not anticipate are recorded here, because each was a real defect or a real constraint rather than a change of mind.

The AI could not afford the roster at all

Found by N2's naval soak: twenty-four days on a board with a port each and not one hull laid down. The cause was neither naval nor new — the planner spent whatever it held every turn, so its treasury never passed about eleven thousand and every unit above that was unbuyable. N1's aircraft were already affected: the only one that had ever appeared was the 9 000 B-Copter, never a fighter or a bomber. Production now compares candidates across every facility rather than taking the first, banks a turn when a better unit is close (save_up_turns), and charges each copy already fielded places in the priority list (duplicate_priority_cost) so the strongest thing a base makes cannot win every decision while a port and an airfield sit idle.

Banking costs first-move fairness, and the trade is measured

The same fix hands whoever moves first a timing edge: they cross a price threshold a turn earlier. Measured over 864 matches on the two original fixtures — first-side bias +5.6 pp with no banking, +14.9 at a two-turn window, +20.2 at three. Two is shipped, because both soaks build their full rosters there and three buys nothing. The same runs showed the commander win-rate spread tightening (52 pp to 38 pp, six warnings to four) against a better-playing AI. No commander .tres was touched: the out-of-band names are the same ones the base game already had, so that is a base-game tuning pass and not this one. Full numbers in docs/commander_balance.md.

One port is one dock doing two jobs

The Straits shipped with two ports a side and eight more cities than drafted. With a single port the fleet came home to refuel and parked on top of the yard that would have replaced it — the port was occupied 73% of the time and naval production simply never happened. The balance fixture had the mirror-image problem: its first version separated the armies with water and ground to the day cap in 430 of 432 matches, so it measured the clock rather than the doctrines. A centred lake fixed it, and it is now the fairest of the three boards at 0.0 pp.

R1 still stands. The AI never builds transports, because it still cannot plan a load-move-unload across turns. Both shipped naval boards are designed around that: the strait is one body of water and the land wraps around it, so fleets and armies each reach the enemy without a ferry. Ferry planning remains the named stretch goal.
