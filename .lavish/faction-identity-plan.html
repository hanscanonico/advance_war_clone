Faction Identity — Armies Wear Their Colours

Pick a Verdant League general today and your army marches in Meridian red under the name "Red Army." The game has four named factions with locked colour themes, yet every match is fought by "Red" versus "Blue" — two of those factions' colours, promoted to side colours and pasted over everyone else. This plan makes a side's name and colours follow its commander's faction everywhere the player looks, while "red/blue" survives only as developer slot vocabulary. It is presentation identity and nothing else — no line of core/ or ai/ ever learns a faction's colour, so rules, saves, replays and the Balance Lab are untouchable by construction.

Meridian Coalition — red Iron Dominion — slate Aurora Compact — blue Verdant League — green
1

The seams already exist

No new architecture is needed — half the game already answers with faction, and the half that says "Red/Blue" funnels through a handful of named authorities:

The sim never knew about red or blue

core/game_state.gd:7 speaks in team ints (TEAMS = [1, 2]), and so do saves, maps, commands and the AI. The whole red/blue identity is presentation. Nothing under core/ or ai/ changes in this plan — the same structural-safety argument the game-speed plan ran on.

The colour authority already exists — it's being violated

scenes/common/commander_visuals.gd is the declared single authority on the four faction themes ("faction first, never colour alone"). Yet commander_select_panel.gd:28 and commander_info_sheet.gd:16 both hardcode RED_TEAM/BLUE_TEAM consts that are literal copies of the Meridian and Aurora colours — the exact duplication that file's header forbids. Even the tile generators' TEAM_COLORS (#d84a3c/#3c64d8) are near-copies of those two hues. This plan deletes the copies and finishes the thought.

The naming authority is one dictionary

terrain_panel.gd:7's TEAM_NAMES is the de facto authority: the day banner (battle.gd:601), the HUD line (battle_view.gd:288), the winner line (battle.gd:713) and the terrain panel's owner labels all ask it — six sites, one dictionary. Renaming the sides is retargeting one authority, not a string hunt.

Half the UI already got this right

The commander HUD chip, the power banner and the victory screen's faction line (battle.gd:747) already tint by CommanderVisuals.theme_for. The board and the side labels are the parts still catching up — win as Verdant and the victory screen says "Verdant League" in green over a board full of red units.

Team colour becomes a row number in exactly two lines

unit_sprite.gd:58 samples the units atlas at team * SPRITE_PX; battle_view.gd:123 does the same for the five team-tinted properties (HQ, city, base, port, airport). Those two lines are where a team int turns into paint — reroute them through a resolved row and the whole board follows.

The tinted art is scripted, not hand-painted

Every team-coloured pixel is pipeline output: build_pixvoxel_atlases.sh picks pre-coloured PixVoxel palettes per row (and already machine-desaturates the white masters for the neutral row), generate_tiles.gd and generate_unit_placeholders.gd paint from a TEAM_COLORS table, and paste_unit_sprites.gd pastes one vendored PNG per unit per row. Adding rows is a pipeline change. The only real art cost is a palette swap of the eight hand-authored air/naval sprites.

2

The identity rules

One resolver, run once at match setup, answers "what does side N look like and what is it called?" for the whole match. Every surface — atlas row, select chip, day banner, terrain panel, victory screen — reads the resolved identity and nothing else.

Meridian #db4a3b
Red — the hue today's "Red Army" already wears, because the select panel's RED_TEAM const was copied from this faction. Its atlas row is today's row 1, byte for byte.
Iron Dominion #4a5258
Cold blue-slate. A new atlas row, tinted from the PixVoxel white masters the way the neutral row already is. The one grey-on-grey readability question is named in R2.
Aurora Compact #3865d8
Blue — today's "Blue Army" hue, copied from this faction. Its atlas row is today's row 2, byte for byte.
Verdant League #2c8636
Green — the faction the current scheme fails hardest: no side colour today is even close. A new atlas row, same tint pipeline as Iron.

How a side resolves

casecolourname
commander, no clash The commander's faction theme. The faction — "Iron Dominion", "Verdant League".
mirror faction Faction sides claim their themes in slot order; a later side whose theme is already taken borrows the first classic theme — Aurora blue, else Meridian red — that is hue-distinct from what's on the board. Iron v Iron → slate + blue; Aurora v Aurora → blue + red. Both keep the faction name; the surfaces that must tell them apart lead with the slot numeral and commander the chips already carry — "1 · Mara Voss".
no commander Generic sides resolve after faction sides and take the first classic theme not in use — so a commanderless match looks exactly like today (red v blue), and a generic side never steals a faction's colour. "First Army" / "Second Army".
dev tooling "Red/Blue" survives as slot vocabulary: the Balance Lab's --red=/--blue= grammar and its byte-stable reports, the --co-select=red|blue capture flag, the scenario driver's comments, and watch mode's diffable team %d print. None of it is player-facing; none of it changes.

Resolution is deterministic and total: faction sides first in slot order, then generic sides, every collision falling to the first hue-distinct classic theme. Two sides can never resolve to the same colour, and the same picks always produce the same board.

3

Decisions

D1 · Identity is presentation-only locked

The resolver lives in scenes/common/ beside CommanderVisuals — the sim keeps its team ints, the save format and map files are untouched, and nothing under core/ or ai/ gains an import, a colour or a name. Identity is derived at scene setup from the match's commander picks and never stored: a resumed save re-resolves to the same answer because the picks are the same. This is the game-speed plan's safety argument reused: the sim cannot disagree with the theatre about who is who, because it never hears the question.

D2 · One resolver, resolved once, read everywhere locked

A side's theme, display name and atlas row are answered by a single authority (scenes/common/side_identity.gd), resolved once per match. TerrainPanel.TEAM_NAMES' six call sites keep asking one authority — its answer changes. The duplicated RED_TEAM/BLUE_TEAM consts in the select panel and info sheet are deleted, not updated: they violate the CommanderVisuals single-authority rule today, independent of this plan. Rejected: resolving lazily per surface — the mirror fallback is a cross-side decision, and two surfaces resolving independently could disagree about who wears the fallback.

D3 · Mirror matches fall back by hue, never by shade locked

When both sides resolve to one faction, the later slot borrows the first hue-distinct classic theme (Aurora blue, else Meridian red) — it keeps the faction name, and the UI leans on the slot numeral and commander to tell the sides apart. Why hue and not a light/dark variant of the faction colour: brightness is already a state channel — exhausted units drop to a 0.55 grey modulate (unit_sprite.gd:13) and submerged subs go half-alpha — so a value-split army would collide with the game's own state cues at 16 px under fog. Team identity must live in the hue channel because the value channel is taken. Rejected: forbidding same-faction picks — two Iron doctrines against each other is a legitimate matchup and the Balance Lab runs mirrors constantly. Within a match there are only two sides, so "Iron wearing Aurora blue" is unambiguous: there is no actual Aurora army on the board to confuse it with.

D4 · Commanderless sides are "First/Second Army" in the classic colours locked

A side playing the neutral commander has no faction to borrow, so it gets a generic slot identity — "First Army" / "Second Army" — painted in the first classic theme not already claimed by a faction side. Generic sides resolve after faction sides so they can never steal a faction's colour: no-CO versus Meridian gives Meridian its red and the generic side blue. A match with no commanders at all renders exactly as today. Rejected: keeping the "Red Army"/"Blue Army" names for this case — the point of the plan is that no player-facing surface says red or blue again.

D5 · Red/blue survives as developer slot vocabulary locked

The Balance Lab's --red=/--blue= side grammar (battle_setup.gd:80), its report vocabulary, the --co-select=red|blue capture flag and the scenario driver's comments all keep saying red/blue — they name slots, not armies, in a headless instrument. This is not laziness but the balance-simulator plan's merge bar: two committed reports rest on byte-stable output, so the Lab's text may not churn. Watch mode's diffable winner line already prints team %d (battle.gd:726) and is untouched. The boundary is crisp: if a player can see it in the game, it speaks faction; if it's a CLI flag or a report, it speaks slots.

4

Milestones — each one shippable

FI1 · The atlas grows faction rows, invisible by default size M

Pipeline only. Both atlases go from 3 rows to 5 — neutral, meridian, aurora, iron, verdant — while the board still samples rows 1 and 2, so the game renders identically and the new art is provably inert. The row order is a deliberate compatibility artifact: Meridian and Aurora keep today's authored red and blue rows byte for byte, and only the resolver (FI2) will ever know the mapping.

  • build_pixvoxel_atlases.sh — two new ROW_PALETTE entries tinting the white color1 masters to Iron slate and Verdant green, the same ImageMagick trick the neutral row's desaturate already plays; applies to the nine land-unit columns and the city/base/HQ property columns alike.
  • generate_tiles.gd + generate_unit_placeholders.gd — ROWS 3 → 5, TEAM_COLORS extended with the two new hues, sourced to match CommanderVisuals' values so the board and the UI can never disagree about what colour a faction is.
  • paste_unit_sprites.gd — TEAM_ROWS gains iron and verdant; the 16 new source PNGs (8 air/naval units × 2 rows) are produced by a scripted exact-value palette swap of the vendored red row, vendored beside the others under iso_air_sea/, and covered by the same --check preflight so make tiles proves the paste before the destructive ground step runs.
  • Proof: make tiles; rows 0–2 of both atlases byte-identical to today; make screenshot unchanged; make verify green.

FI2 · The resolver, and the board obeys it size M

Where the feature becomes visible: pick Verdant and a green army called Verdant League fights on screen.

  • scenes/common/side_identity.gd — the §2 rules as one resolver: {team → theme, display name, atlas row}, computed once at match setup from the sides' commanders, with the D3/D4 fallback order inside.
  • unit_sprite.gd — texture_for takes the resolved row instead of computing team * SPRITE_PX; the sprite keeps unit.team for all logic (acted grey-out, fog) — only texture selection changes. Menus previewing unit art route through the same call.
  • battle_view.gd:123 — property rows come from the resolver; the runtime TileSet registers rows 1–4 for the five team-tinted terrains.
  • terrain_panel.gd — TEAM_NAMES becomes a read of the resolver, which retargets all six naming sites (day banner, HUD, winner line, owner labels) in one move.
  • Proof: Verdant pick plays green end to end; an Iron mirror renders slate v blue; a commanderless match is pixel-identical to today; make verify green (the resolver is Node-free and gets a unit test for the full §2 resolution table, including both collision orders).

FI3 · The selection page and info sheet speak faction size S

The last player-facing red/blue strings, plus the payoff detail: the select chips become a live preview of the colours the match will actually wear.

  • commander_select_panel.gd — chips read "1 · Meridian Coalition — You" style labels and tint from the resolver, updating live as picks change — including showing the mirror fallback the moment both players land on one faction, so the D3 rule is visible before the match starts, not a surprise on day 1. The duplicated RED_TEAM/BLUE_TEAM consts are deleted.
  • commander_info_sheet.gd — the "RED ARMY / BLUE ARMY" cards title and tint by resolved identity; its duplicated consts deleted too.
  • README — a short note on faction colours and the mirror rule.
  • Proof: play menu → victory with each fallback case; a grep -riE "red|blue" scenes/ audit finds only dev flags, comments and the resolver's classic-theme table — no player-facing string.
5

Risks

R1 · Machine-tinted rows read worse than the authored palettes

Today's red and blue rows are hand-authored PixVoxel palettes; the Iron and Verdant rows are ImageMagick tints of the white masters. If a tinted tank reads as mud at 16 px, the named fallback is to vendor the pack's real green and dark palettes — the source URL sits in the build script's header, the vendoring pattern already exists, and nothing downstream changes because the pipeline only cares that each row has art. The neutral row is precedent that a machine pass over these masters can look deliberate.

R2 · Iron slate versus neutral grey on the board

Neutral properties are a desaturated #8a9099; Iron is #4a5258. Distinct in value and warmth, but both are greys, and this is the one place the plan itself leans on a value difference. Contained: units are never neutral, properties never grey out, so the only possible meeting is Iron pieces beside unowned properties. Mitigation: keep the Iron row cold and dark and the neutral row warm and light; check in a played match at 1× zoom; if it still smudges, the Iron hue is one pipeline constant, and the terrain panel names the owner as the backstop.

R3 · Capture gallery churn

Scenario captures that stage commanders will recolour wherever a staged side now wears its faction — a one-time, expected diff reviewed by eye. No scenario logic breaks: the driver waits on scene states, not pixels, and watch mode's diffable line prints team ints. The commanderless captures are the control group — they must not change by a byte.

R4 · The Balance Lab and its merge bar

Low by construction: nothing under tools/balance/ is touched, the --red/--blue grammar and report text are frozen by D5, and the fixed-seed byte-diff of both reports is the proof, exactly as the balance-simulator plan's merge bar demands.

R5 · Determinism, saves, replays

Structurally safe rather than carefully safe, as with game speed: no core/ or ai/ file gains an import or a field, state carries team ints everywhere, and identity is a pure function of the match's commander picks — re-derived on load, never stored, so there is no second copy of the truth to drift.

6

Explicitly out of scope

A free army-colour picker

The Advance-Wars-style "pick any colour for your slot." The whole point here is that identity follows the faction; a picker could layer onto the resolver seam later, but it is a different feature with its own UI bill.

On-board ownership markers beyond colour

Emblem pips or shape cues on units — the colour-blind-safe answer the "never colour alone" principle points at. Worth doing, pre-existing gap (red v blue is hue-only today too), and it deserves its own decision rather than riding this plan.

Renaming the Lab's vocabulary

Locked out by D5 and the balance-simulator merge bar: the --red/--blue grammar and report text stay byte-stable. Slots are slots.

More than two sides, faction-locked rosters

The resolver is written against the two-team sim that exists, and commanders stay pickable by either side — no "faction locks its generals" rule enters with this plan.
