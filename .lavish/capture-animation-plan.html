Grid Commander · Battle presentation · Design of record

The capture cut-in — mash, flip, banner

When an infantry squad takes a property, the board gives way to a single-panel cut-in: the squad marches up to the building, hops on it — squash, dust, a -4 chip per landing — while a capture-points meter drains, and on completion the building flashes white and flips to the capturing faction's colours under a CAPTURED! banner, exactly as Advance Wars staged it. It is the combat cut-in's sibling in every structural sense: one awaited method on BattleAnimator, one clock, one snapshot from the sim — it replays, it never decides. The motion reference is the design-system handoff's capture-scene.jsx; the timings in this plan are that reference retimed to this game's tempo.

0 Already landed — every property is now presentable

The cut-in's centrepiece is the property drawn at 64 px, and until this session two of the five capturables couldn't do it: the airport and the port were flat 16 px programmer art drawn whole by tools/generate_tiles.gd, fine on a tile and wrong blown up beside the PixVoxel city, base and HQ. That prerequisite shipped with this plan, as working tree changes ready to commit:

  • assets/sprites/iso_buildings/ — hand-authored 64 px isometric airport and port buildings from the design-system handoff (project-original, drawn to match the PixVoxel style), neutral/red/blue committed, iron/verdant derived by the same masked-HSB run as the air/naval sprites (tools/tint_iso_air_sea.sh).
  • tools/build_pixvoxel_atlases.sh now composites them into terrain-atlas columns 9–10 over rebuilt grounds (asphalt apron, open water), per team row, with the ten sources asserted by --check; generate_tiles.gd draws those cells as bare grounds, exactly like the city/base/hq lots.
  • All five faction rows verified in the atlas and in-game on the_straits; every other atlas byte — units, overlay, cursor, icon, the other twelve terrain columns — rebuilt byte-identical. assets/LICENSES.md and the README record the new art class.

Consequence for this plan: the property sprite the cut-in shows is the board's own atlas cell — any capturable terrain, any faction row — with no special cases.

1 The seams already exist

Capture presentation has exactly two call sites, and both already sit in an awaited flow: the player's &"capture" branch and the AI runner's CaptureCommand branch each run command.apply(game) and then patch the board up inline — repaint, refresh, HUD. There is no animation between apply and repaint today; the cut-in is one awaited call inserted at that seam, and the AI gets it for free because the AI issues the same commands the player does. Almost everything else the feature needs already has a single authority to ask.

The one thing missing: CaptureCommand.apply returns nothing — by the time any animation could run, capture_progress has already moved and the owner may have flipped. So the command grows a result snapshot, exactly as AttackCommand already carries one: points_before, points_after, captured, filled inside apply and covered by a core unit test. That is the entire core change. Everything else in this plan lives in the presentation layer.

2 What plays — anatomy of the cut-in

A single centered panel wipes in over the darkened board, wearing the same letterbox chrome as the combat cut-in. Top plate: the unit's name and a gold CAPTURE tag. Arena: sky, far hills, a ground plane; the property sprite center-right in its current owner's colours; a squad of three figures marching in from the left with a bob. Bottom plate: the property's name and defence stars. Top-right, the capture-points meter — a big N/20 numeral over twenty tick pips, posed at points_before. Then the mash: the squad hops onto the building, and every landing squashes it, kicks dust, shakes the camera, thumps the capture sample, and floats a -N chip while the meter drains. If points_after is zero, the building flashes white, swaps to the capturing team's atlas row at the flash peak, and a CAPTURED! banner lands with celebration specks; otherwise the banner reads OCCUPYING — N/20 LEFT. Wipe out, and the board — repainted while hidden — agrees with everything just shown.

The beat sheet

Budgets below are the Normal tier; every one is a GameSpeed accessor scaled per tier like the rest of the game's motion. Unskipped it runs ≈ 2.4 s for a completing capture and ≈ 2.0 s for a partial one. The handoff reference plays the same choreography in 4.6 s; it is deliberately retimed here, because captures are the most frequent ceremony in the game — a 20-point property is two turns of mashing minimum — and the combat cut-in already set the house tempo at ≈ 2 s (its R1). Any press at any beat fast-forwards the clock to the end — the combat cut-in's law, never a tween abort — so the await resolves exactly once.

BeatBudgetDriven byWhat shows
1 · Wipe in + set0.25 s—panel slides over the darkened board; plates slide in; meter posed at points_before; property in the previous owner's row

6 · Wipe out0.25 s—reverse wipe; underneath, the board was already repainted and refreshed, so the return frame agrees with the banner

The mash count and the chips are a presentation split of the one number the sim committed: hops = min(3, points_before − points_after), with the removed points divided across the hops largest-first (a strength-4 turn chips 2/1/1; a strength-12 doctrine turn chips 4/4/4). The chips sum to the removed points by construction — the cut-in never calls capture_strength, never reads the doctrine, and a mid-animation press lands on the same meter value the terrain panel will report.

The call-site surgery — all of it

# battle.gd &"capture" branch and battle_ai_runner.gd CaptureCommand branch, identically:
command.apply(game)
EventBus.unit_moved.emit(unit)
await animator.animate_capture(command.result, unit, dest)   # the cut-in; CP1
if game.owner_at(dest) == unit.team:
	EventBus.property_captured.emit(dest, unit.team)
	view.repaint_property(dest)
view.refresh_sprite(unit)
# ... then exactly what each site already does: selection/panel/HUD, victory check.

animate_capture gates like animate_combat does: cut-in only when the toggle is on, not capturing, not Instant, and the viewer can see the capturing unit — one unit this time, standing on the very cell it captures, so a single view.can_see_unit answers for both. When the gate refuses, the method plays the on-map fallback — a single capture thump if the viewer can see the cell, plus the flag-pip pulse CP1 adds to the tile — and returns; under fog an enemy's hidden capture stays exactly as silent and invisible as it is today. Repaint and the property_captured emission move after the await: under the full-screen panel the board is hidden, so the flip the player watches in the cut-in is the flip the board wears when it returns; on the refused path the await returns immediately and the order is today's, unchanged.

3 Decisions

D1 — The cut-in replays a snapshot; it computes nothing

CaptureCommand gains a result the way AttackCommand already has one: points_before, points_after, captured, filled inside apply at the moment the sim commits them, unit-tested in core. The cut-in reads the result, the unit, and the cell — it never touches capture_strength, the doctrine hooks, or capture_progress. The battle-animations plan's D1 said why once and it holds twice: this repo has already paid for a second independent opinion (movement), and the animation layer will not open that door for capture.

D2 — Board art, blown up; the flip is an atlas-row swap

The property sprite is the terrain atlas's own 64 px cell — (terrain.atlas_col, SideIdentity.atlas_row(owner)) — and completion swaps the row from the previous owner's to the capturer's at the flash peak. No second palette, no bespoke building art: whatever the board would show, the cut-in shows bigger, which is what section 0's sprite work bought. Squad figures are UnitSprite.texture_for — infantry and mech are the only capturers, both PixVoxel art. Squash, dust, chips, specks and flash are procedural, in the combat cut-in's effect idiom.

D3 — One awaited seam on BattleAnimator, one clock behind it

animate_capture(result, unit, cell) joins animate_combat as the second full-screen seam: both call sites await it, it returns exactly once, and the scene behind it — scenes/battle/cutscene/capture_cutscene.gd beside the combat one — is a pure function of a single clock. Skipping sets the clock to the end; nothing is cancelled mid-flight, so any press at any beat lands on the right tableau by construction, not by testing. Board repaint and the property_captured emission move to after the await (section 2) — the one deliberate reordering, and invisible on every path where the cut-in doesn't play.

D4 — The combat gate, reused whole; no new toggle

One switch governs full-screen theatre: Settings.battle_animations covers this cut-in too, and the gate is _cut_in_applies' four questions with one unit instead of two — toggle, capturing (screenshots stay byte-stable), Settings.speed.instant (that tier exists to skip theatre), and viewer visibility asked of the view, and so of core/rules/vision.gd, never re-derived. A player who turned battle cut-ins off has answered this question already; a second checkbox would be a setting nobody asked for.

D5 — Pacing belongs to GameSpeed; core stays deaf to it

Every budget in the beat sheet is a constant on GameSpeed read at play time, scaled per tier like the move step and the banners — Slow lingers, Quick tightens, Instant never enters the scene at all (the explicit branch, per the game-speed plan's D4). The standing invariant stands: nothing under core/ or ai/ imports GameSpeed or reads Settings; the snapshot fields are plain ints and the sim neither knows nor cares whether its capture was watched.

D6 — The dormant sound and signal get their jobs

The pooled-but-unplayed capture sample becomes the mash thump (one per landing) and the on-map fallback's single tick; fanfare — already the victory voice — marks the CAPTURED! banner. property_captured keeps its shape and both emitters, just moved after the await, and stays the seam any future listener (minimap, replay ticker) subscribes to. Missing streams stay silently skipped, as Sfx already guarantees.

4 Milestones — each one shippable

CP1

The snapshot and the shell — every capture cuts in, readable, skippable

  • Core: CaptureCommand.result (points_before / points_after / captured) set in apply; tests prove the snapshot against chip-away, completion, doctrine-boosted (Tomás Reed) and floor-of-1 captures, and that save/load neither needs nor keeps it.
  • Scene: capture_cutscene.tscn/.gd — wipe, plates, property sprite by atlas cell, points meter ticking before → after in one beat (no mash choreography yet), both banners, wipe out; play/skip/pose_at/finished contract identical to the combat scene's.
  • Wiring: animate_capture on the animator with the reused gate; both call sites resequenced per section 2; on-map fallback thump; a flag-pip pulse on the tile for the gate-refused-but-visible case.
  • Ships: captures feel announced from day one; toggle off restores today's behaviour exactly.
CP2

The mash — march, hops, squash, dust, chips, flip

  • Squad march-in with bob; 1–3 hops from the removed-points split, each landing with squash, dust puff, decaying shake, capture thump and a floating -N chip synced to the meter.
  • Completion flip: white flash, atlas-row swap at the peak, celebration specks; partial keeps the plain OCCUPYING banner. Chip split verified against strength 1, 4, 10 and a doctrine-boosted 12.
  • Ships: the full choreography of the reference, at house tempo, for all five property types and all five faction rows.
CP3

Pace and polish — the ceremony survives a whole match

  • Tier budgets on GameSpeed tuned by playing a property-heavy board at each tier; AI-turn compression via the animator's existing recent-cut-in pacing so a three-capture computer turn doesn't triple the ceremony.
  • The HQ handoff: a capture that sets game.winner plays its CAPTURED! beat first, then the victory flow enters — one ceremony after the other, never stacked.
  • Scenario driver gains a posed capture (pose_at) for screenshots; make screenshot stays byte-identical.
  • Verification soak: a full AI-vs-AI match with animations on — captures, doctrine bonuses, an HQ finish; no softlock, meter always equal to the terrain panel's number; then the same match with the toggle off (R2, R5).

5 Risks

R1 · Ceremony fatigue — captures happen far more often than kills

A 20-point property is at least two mash turns, an infantry rush is a dozen captures a match, and every one now wants two seconds of theatre. Mitigations are layered and mostly inherited: skip on any press, the shared toggle, house-tempo budgets from day one (not the reference's 4.6 s), and CP3's AI-turn compression. Held in reserve as a plan amendment if the CP3 soak still grates: partial captures demote to the on-map pulse and only completions earn the panel.

R2 · The await contract — a cut-in that never finishes is a softlock

Both call sites hold the whole interaction flow on animate_capture, exactly as combat does. Same design answer, because it is proven here: one clock, skip fast-forwards state rather than cancelling tweens, every branch funnels to one finished. Verified by CP3's soak plus deliberate skip-spamming at every beat, including the frame of the row swap.

R3 · A second opinion on capture math

The tempting bug: the cut-in "just quickly" calling capture_strength to size its chips, then drifting from the sim the day a doctrine changes. D1 is the fence — chips are a split of points_before − points_after and nothing else — and the acceptance check pins it: chip sum equals the snapshot delta for every doctrine in the roster, including Tomás Reed's boosted turns.

R4 · Event resequencing — the one behavioural edit outside the cut-in

Moving repaint and property_captured after the await is the only change visible to code that never shows a cut-in, and the rules here live in two layers (the memory this repo keeps: unit tests miss that split). Answer: the refused-gate path returns immediately so ordering is byte-identical today, and CP3's AI-vs-AI soak — the instrument that catches split-layer bugs here — runs with the toggle in both positions before this merges.

R5 · Screenshot determinism

Already solved by inheritance: the gate refuses while capturing, exactly like the combat cut-in, the shake and the pulse, so make screenshot and the smoke scenarios stay byte-stable; a posed capture screenshot goes through pose_at at a settled beat, the combat scene's precedent.

R6 · Fog — the cut-in must not out-see the viewer

Handled at the gate, one question this time: the capturing unit stands on the captured cell, so view.can_see_unit answers for the whole scene, and the view asks core/rules/vision.gd — the single authority, no new visibility logic anywhere. A hidden enemy's capture stays as silent and invisible as it is today.

6 Explicitly out of scope

  • Build / resupply / join cutscenes — this plan is capture only, as the battle-animations plan was combat only; the on-map language for everything else stays.
  • Authored mash animation frames — squash, dust and chips carry the choreography; per-unit capture sprite sheets are the same scope-creep trap the combat plan fenced off (its R5), fenced identically here.
  • Commander portraits in the capture frame — the power banner owns commander presentation; a doctrine-boosted mash reads through bigger chips, not a face.
  • Replacing the terrain panel's capture readout — the panel's N/20 stays the exact-number authority between turns; the meter is the same number, staged.
  • Reworking the handoff's HP-cost model — the reference narrates "one action removes the unit's HP"; this game's truth is capture_strength (HP × doctrine, floor 1), and the cut-in simply replays whatever the sim committed.

7 Acceptance checks

  • Every capture — player or AI, partial or completing — plays the cut-in when the gate allows it; toggle off, Instant, capturing, or a fog-hidden capturer reproduces today's behaviour exactly.
  • Meter values, chips, hop count and the banner always equal the sim's snapshot: chip sum = points_before − points_after for every doctrine in the roster; the terrain panel agrees the moment the wipe clears. No capture number is computed in the presentation layer.
  • Any press at any beat — including mid-flash — lands on the correct final tableau and returns control immediately; skip-spamming a full AI turn of captures never softlocks.
  • The completion flip shows the capturer's faction row via SideIdentity, for all five property types and all five rows — including a mirror match and a commander-less side.
  • An HQ capture plays CAPTURED! to the end, then the victory flow begins; the two ceremonies never overlap.
  • make test stays green including the new snapshot tests; make screenshot output is unchanged with the feature merged; no file under core/ or ai/ references a Node, a scene, GameSpeed or Settings.
