Grid Commander · Stretch item · Shipped · rev 1

Game Speed — Slow · Normal · Quick · Instant

Units currently cross a tile in 0.06 s — a full infantry move is over in the time of a blink, and an AI turn is a shower of teleports. This plan adds a game-speed setting: a device preference that scales how fast moves and battles play out on screen, pickable on the main menu and switchable mid-match. It is presentation pacing and nothing else — no line of core/ or ai/ ever learns the speed, so outcomes, saves, replays and determinism are untouchable by construction.

Slow — every step readable Normal — the new default Quick — today's game, bit for bit Instant — results, not theatre
1

The seams already exist

No new architecture is needed. Every second the battle scene ever waits is already a named constant, and both sides of the match animate through the same code:

Nine numbers, three files — that is the whole inventory

A sweep of every create_tween / create_timer in the presentation layer finds all gameplay pacing here: battle_animator.gd (move step 0.06, turn banner 1.2, power banner 1.1, camera shake, cursor pulse), unit_sprite.gd (hit flash 0.08/0.12, death fade 0.25), battle_ai_runner.gd (turn-open delay, 0.2 between AI commands). The only other tween in the game is a button fade on the commander HUD chip — feedback, not pacing, and deliberately left alone.

The AI already animates through the player's pipes

battle_ai_runner.gd:10 promises that an AI turn "resolves and animates exactly as a player's does" — it calls the same animate_path / animate_combat. Scale the animator once and both flows follow; there is no second animation system to keep in sync.

"Suppress the animation" already has a precedent

The animator's capturing flag (battle_animator.gd:29) already skips the camera shake and cursor pulse so screenshots settle. Instant is that idea generalised: an explicit "don't tween, just show the result" branch, not a zero multiplied through the maths.

The tooling was written for this day

battle_scenario_driver.gd:372 says it outright: scenarios wait on the scene's state machine rather than frame counts "so they stay correct when animation timings change." The menu's difficulty dropdown is the model for the picker, and core/save_game.gd shows the user:// pattern the new settings file follows.

2

What each tier changes

A tier is two knobs and one flag: an animation scale (movement, hit flash, death fade), a pacing scale (the AI's think-beats), and instant (skip the tweens entirely). Banners are information, not theatre — they hold at a readable length at every tier and only tighten under Instant. The camera shake and cursor pulse never scale: one is impact feedback, the other is idle UI.

Slow anim ×3
Every step of a path is individually readable — 0.18 s a tile. For learning the game, watching a doctrine play out, or showing someone what just happened. AI think-beats stretch a little so its turn reads as decisions, not a slideshow.
Normal anim ×2 · default
The new default, and the fix for the complaint that started this plan: 0.12 s a tile, twice today's duration. Movement registers as movement; combat still snaps. Everything else paces as the game always has.
Quick anim ×1
Today's game, bit for bit — every constant at its current value. Nobody who likes the present feel loses anything: it is one dropdown away, and the plumbing milestone ships with this as the default to prove the routing changed nothing.
Instant snap
No tweens: sprites appear at their destinations, casualties vanish, sounds still fire so actions register. Banners tighten to a 0.5 s hold — a day change must still be noticeable. The AI turn runs at one command per frame, so the board repaints and the window stays live. For grinding out the late game.

3

Decisions

D1 · What kind of setting is this? locked

A device preference, not match state. A new autoload/settings.gd singleton owns it, persists it to user://settings.cfg (Godot's ConfigFile, beside SaveGame's user://save.json), and answers Settings.speed to anyone in the presentation layer. It does not ride MatchConfig and is not written into saves: resuming a three-day-old save should play at the speed you like today, and a hot-seat pair share one screen anyway. A --speed=<tier> CLI flag overrides for one run without persisting, in the family of --map / --fog / --difficulty. As built, the flag also outranks the tier a capture pins itself to — it is the most specific thing anyone said, and asking for a capture of a tier is how you look at one you are tuning. The pin itself lives on Settings rather than on the animator, because the in-battle menu row reads its label from there too: pinning only the animator would have produced a frame whose animations were machine-independent and whose text was not. Rejected: Engine.time_scale — a global hammer that would also speed menus, the cursor pulse, and anything physics ever touches.

D2 · Where do the numbers live? locked

As constants on a GameSpeed presentation class (scenes/common/game_speed.gd: id, display name, the two scales, the instant flag, plus by_id / ordered / next for the cycling menu row) — not as .tres files under data/. This deliberately breaks the difficulty-tier symmetry, for a principled reason: data/ is for numbers the sim reads, and the entire safety argument of this feature is that the sim cannot read the speed. Pacing constants already live beside the animations they time; they stay there, just gathered into one table.

D3 · Which tier is today's game — and which is the default? locked

Quick reproduces today bit for bit; Normal becomes the default at twice the movement duration. The complaint that opened this plan is about the current default, so the default is what moves — but the current feel stays one dropdown away, and the plumbing milestone ships with Quick as the default first, precisely so "nothing changed" is checkable before anything does change. The mid-match menu row means a wrong guess about Normal costs the player two clicks, not a restart.

D4 · What exactly does Instant do? locked

An explicit branch, in the tradition of capturing — not animation scale 0. animate_path sets the sprite's position and returns (the flow already tolerates a same-frame return: one-cell "moves" take that path today, battle.gd:273). Combat skips flash, fade and shake but keeps its sounds — an attack you triggered must still register. Banners clamp to a 0.5 s hold rather than vanishing: whose day it is remains information. The AI runner drops its delays but still awaits one process_frame per command, so a 40-command turn is forty repaints the eye can track as a fast flicker, the window keeps pumping events, and the per-turn safety cap keeps its meaning.

4

Milestones — each one shippable

GS1 · The speed authority, invisible by default size S shipped

All four tiers exist and every duration routes through them — with the default pinned to Quick, so the game plays identically to today and the routing is provably inert.

  • scenes/common/game_speed.gd — the tier table (§2's numbers), lookup and cycling helpers.
  • autoload/settings.gd — loads/saves user://settings.cfg, applies a --speed= override un-persisted; registered in project.godot (minimal, reviewable hand edit).
  • Route all nine sites: the animator's move step and banner holds, the sprite's flash/fade (durations handed in by the animator — the sprite stays dumb, deriving nothing but sim state), the runner's two delays. The Instant branches of D4 land here too, reachable only via --speed=instant.
  • Speed is read at each use, never cached at scene start — a mid-match change takes effect on the very next animation.
  • Capture and scenario runs (--screenshot, the scenario driver) pin their own profile and ignore settings.cfg: a screenshot must not depend on which machine took it.
  • Proof: make screenshot byte-identical intent, a played match indistinguishable, make verify green (nothing in tests/ touches presentation — per the testing doctrine, none owed here).

GS2 · The two pickers, then the new default size S shipped

Where a player meets the setting: once in the menu, once mid-match. Only after both exist does the default flip to Normal.

  • Main menu: a "Speed" OptionButton beside Difficulty, writing Settings immediately — it is a device preference, so it deliberately does not flow through _start / MatchConfig. Tooltip in the house style: "How fast moves and battles play out on screen. Never changes an outcome — pacing only. Changeable any time from the in-battle menu."
  • Battle: a Speed: Normal row on the map action menu (battle_menus.gd · map_actions), cycling Slow → Normal → Quick → Instant on choose and persisting. The row's label asks Settings — the same single-authority gating the rest of that file practices.
  • Flip DEFAULT_ID to normal — the original complaint is now fixed, and undoing it is two clicks, not a code edit.
  • Proof: cycle mid-match and watch the next move obey; quit and relaunch and the choice survives; hot-seat both sides share it.

GS3 · The tooling dividend, and the numbers pass size S shipped — retune outstanding

Spend the new capability on the pipeline, then settle the table with played evidence.

  • Pin captures to Instant: make screenshot and the scenario gallery stop paying for animations they never photograph. The driver already waits on states, not frames (battle_scenario_driver.gd:372) — this is the payoff of that discipline.
  • Play a full match at each tier (including an AI mirror at Instant) and retune §2's starting numbers in the one place they live.
  • README: the setting, the flag, where settings.cfg lives.

As built: the pin and the README landed, and every tier was exercised through the resolve, transport and victory scenarios — wall clock scales monotonically (2.07 / 1.72 / 1.24 / 1.01 s) and all four produce a byte-identical frame, so the "pacing only" claim is measured rather than asserted. Pinning captures to Instant took the whole-AI-turn scenario from 5.95 s to 1.48 s for the same frame. Outstanding: the subjective retune — §2's numbers are still the plan's starting values, characterized but not yet adjusted against a full match played at each tier by a human.

5

Risks

R1 · A future animation bypasses the authority

Someone adds a tween with a literal 0.3 and it ignores the setting forever. Mitigation: after GS1 the review rule is mechanical — no literal seconds in battle-scene tweens or timers; every duration is a GameSpeed field or scaled through one — and a grep create_tween|create_timer across scenes/ is the whole audit, nine sites today.

R2 · Instant turns the AI's move into a magic trick

Forty commands in a blur; the player looks up and the front line has moved. This is what Instant is for, but the plan keeps three tethers: one repaint per command, the cursor still jumps to each acting unit, and banners clamp instead of vanishing. If it still reads as a hang or a cheat in playtest, the honest fast tier is Quick — Instant's numbers are not softened to split the difference.

R3 · Same-frame animation returns surprise a caller

Low: the flow already survives exactly this. A one-cell "move" skips the tween and calls the done-handler synchronously today (battle.gd:273), and the scenario driver waits on states by design. Instant widens a path the code already walks.

R4 · Determinism, saves, replays

Structurally safe rather than carefully safe: no file in core/ or ai/ gains an import, a field, or a read. The save format is untouched, the command log is untouched, and the same seed plus the same commands produce the same result at any speed — the sim cannot observe the clock the theatre runs on.

6

Explicitly out of scope

Global slow-motion

Engine.time_scale and any physics-clock trickery. Rejected in D1: the setting paces the battle's theatre, not the engine.

Hold-to-fast-forward

The genre's other answer (hold a button, the current animation sprints). Genuinely nice, and GS1's routing makes it cheap later — but it is an input feature, not a setting, and this plan ships the setting.

UI transition speeds & audio scaling

Menu fades, the HUD chip's button feedback, the cursor pulse, and any pitch/length change to sound effects. Feedback stays constant; only gameplay theatre scales.

Per-match or per-save speeds

The speed never enters MatchConfig or the save file (D1). One device, one preference, changeable at any moment — there is nothing a second copy of the truth could add except disagreement.
