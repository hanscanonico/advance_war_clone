Production Maps — no armies, only factories

Three new boards — small, medium, big — that hand each commander zero starting units and more bases than any map in the roster. Every fight on them is manufactured: the opening is a build order, the mid-game an income race for neutral cities, and on the two larger boards a race for neutral bases and airports that expand production itself. All three landed as plain maps/*.txt files — the menu auto-discovers them, zero engine code changed.

1 The premise — production as the whole game

All nine shipped maps hand out an army and let production reinforce it. These three invert that: nothing on the board moves on day 1. What you get instead is factories — two to four owned bases a side where the roster tops out at two, plus an owned airport on the two larger boards. The map is the economy. 2 Why an empty board is legal — verified before building

A zero-unit start only works if the rules never mistake "no army yet" for "defeated", and the AI knows to buy before it fights. Both hold, by code that already exists:

Defeat can't fire at start

The winner is only ever set in two places: HQ capture (core/commands/capture_command.gd) and rout in GameState._check_rout — and the rout check is called from one place, remove_unit. No unit ever dies on an empty board, so an armyless day 1 is simply a quiet day, not a loss.

The AI builds first, unprompted

ai/ai_controller.gd::plan_next_command scores unit actions first and falls through to _plan_build when there are none — with zero units that happens immediately, so both sides open with production on day 1. No AI changes needed.

The parser already agrees

In core/map_data.gd the [units] section is optional — starting_units just stays empty and GameState.create skips an empty loop. Day-1 funds come from core/rules/turn_rules.gd: 1,000 per owned property.

3 The three boards

forge · small · 12×10

2 bases a side 8 neutral cities 3,000/turn opening income

"A cramped foundry duel: no starting armies, two bases a side, one river between them."

  • Twice scrimmage's production in nearly the same footprint — the opening is a build-order puzzle, not a march.
  • An S-bend river splits the board; the two bridges sit on the road spine, so every land push funnels through a chokepoint.
  • Each side's nearest neutral city is 2 tiles from a base — a day-1 infantry captures it comfortably inside the soak's 10-day gate.

arsenal · medium · 18×13

3 bases + airport a side 2 neutral bases 10 neutral cities 5,000/turn opening income

"Armies are built, not given: three bases and an airport a side, a neutral forge apiece by the river."

  • A full-width river crossed by exactly two bridges: land forces funnel, and the owned airport is the flanking answer that ignores the river entirely.
  • One neutral base sits just off the river on each half — the first expansion prize, and the fourth factory for whoever claims it.
  • Roads run base cluster → bridge on each side, so vehicles reach the front without detours.

steelworks · big · 26×18 — largest board in the game

4 bases + airport a side 2 neutral bases · 2 neutral airports 18 neutral cities 6,000/turn opening income

"The grand empty board: four bases and an airport a side, and a mountain wall with one gate."

  • A mountain wall crosses the middle with a single 2×2 road gate at dead center — armored columns meet there, and the neutral base pair sits right behind the clash.
  • A neutral airport on each flank rewards whoever wins the wings with air production the mountain wall can't stop.
  • The road ring runs HQ cluster → gate → enemy HQ cluster, so the biggest board still funnels to one readable front.

4 Decisions

D1 · Zero units = an omitted [units] section

Not an empty list, not a flag, no parser change — the section simply isn't there. MapData.parse already treats it as optional.

D2 · Symmetric by construction

All three are exact 180° mirrors — terrain and owners — generated by a script that asserts the mirror before writing, and tagged # symmetric so the map lint enforces it forever. In a game decided purely by production, positional fairness is the whole balance story.

D3 · No naval on these boards

the_straits owns the naval domain, and the standing R1 risk from the naval plan — the AI never builds transports — makes a big naval board its own design problem. Airports deliver the "more production" promise without it.

D4 · Soak-driven city placement

The per-map AI soak demands a capture (or a win) within 10 days. On a no-unit board that clock starts from an empty field, so every side has a neutral city ≤2 tiles from a base. This is a hard test gate, not a style preference.

D5 · Classic opening economy confirmed in review

Only the HQ, bases and the airport start owned; every city is neutral — matching the shipped roster, where income is earned by capture. Opening income is 3k / 5k / 6k per turn across the three boards.

5 Validation — every gate green

Both suites auto-discover the roster via MapCatalog.paths(), so all three maps were tested the moment the files landed. make test: 441/441.

Map lint tests/unit/test_maps.gd

  • Parses and builds a GameState
  • Exactly one owned HQ per team, no spares
  • Each team owns at least one base
  • HQs mutually reachable on foot
  • # symmetric tag verified — terrain and owners mirror
  • Menu description present; smallest-board-first ordering intact

AI soak tests/unit/test_map_soak.gd

Ten AI-vs-AI days on every board, neutral commanders, seeded RNG. The bar: someone wins or at least one property changes hands. On these maps that means the AI proved the whole loop — build from nothing → march → capture — on all three boards, inside the command cap even at 26×18.

6 Risks

R1 · Pacing is designed-in, not playtested

Symmetry makes the boards fair by construction, but nobody has felt the no-unit opening yet — steelworks especially may open slowly with infantry crawling a 26-wide board. Mitigation: a few human games; if it drags, promote the neutral properties closer or raise starting ownership (open question below).

R2 · AI turn time on the biggest board

More factories means more candidate builds and eventually more units to plan for. The soak passed within its command cap, but watch real turn length on steelworks late-game — same watch item the More Maps plan held for ironworks.

R3 · No-unit starts spotlight AI build order

With no gifted army, the AI's opening is pure AIProfile.build_priority. That's by design — and it respects the difficulty plan's locked rule (the AI never cheats; tiers only reweigh the planner). No tier resource files were touched.

7 Review outcome — all three questions resolved

Reviewed 2026-07-22. Every question closed in favor of the shipped design; no map files changed as a result of review.

Q1 → Classic economy stays

Only HQ, bases and the airport start owned; every city is neutral and must be earned. (Now locked as D5.)

Q2 → forge stays lean

No neutral bases on the small board — production complexity is the larger boards' identity; forge stays the quick, readable duel.

Q3 → No naval follow-up now

The trio is the full scope of this work. A port-heavy production board remains a possible future item, to be planned on its own terms against the naval plan's R1.
