Advance Wars Clone in Godot

A turn-based tactics game in the style of Advance Wars (GBA): grid maps, terrain that shapes movement and defense, a rock-paper-scissors unit roster, property capture and income, and eventually a computer opponent. This plan proposes the architecture, the mechanics to implement, and eight milestones that each end in something playable.

1 · Where we are, where we're going

Current state PLAN COMPLETE — M0–M7 done

As of 2026-07-18, all eight milestones are delivered: the game boots to a main menu with map select and a fog-of-war toggle, plays 1P (vs the Blue AI) or hot-seat, supports fog with true vision rules, saves and resumes matches, and has generated sound effects, screen shake, and a real victory screen with Rematch. 143 GUT tests passing. What remains is the stretch list below: CO powers, naval/air units, a map editor, campaign scripting, and online play.

Target (MVP, end of M4)

Two players on one machine play a full match on a hand-made map: move units, fight with real damage-chart combat, capture cities for income, build units at bases, and win by HQ capture or destroying every enemy unit.

Target (full, end of M7)

Single-player vs an AI, full ground roster with transports, fuel and ammo, fog of war, sound, save/load — a complete small game. CO powers, naval/air units, a map editor and online play stay on the stretch list.

Legal note: Advance Wars' mechanics are fine to reimplement, but Nintendo's sprites, music, unit names in combination with the trade dress, and the "Advance Wars" name are not. The plan assumes original or freely-licensed assets (see the art decision below) and a different title.

2 · Proposed technical decisions

Four decisions shape everything else. Each is a proposal — the ones worth debating are also surfaced as questions in section 7.

Godot 4.4+ · GDScript

Latest stable Godot 4. GDScript over C#: first-class docs and examples, zero build step, fastest iteration for a solo gameplay-heavy project. Nothing here is performance-bound enough to need C#. Typed GDScript everywhere (class_name, typed vars) keeps refactors safe.

Simulation / presentation split

The game state (map, units, funds, turn) lives in pure GDScript classes with no Node dependencies. Scenes only render state and animate changes. This makes rules unit-testable, lets the AI simulate moves cheaply, and keeps the door open for replays and online play later.

Everything data-driven via Resources

Unit stats, terrain properties, and the damage chart are custom Resource files (.tres), not constants in code. Balancing means editing data in the inspector; adding a unit means adding a file. The damage chart is a single resource holding the attacker × defender base-damage matrix.

Command pattern for all actions

Move, Attack, Capture, Build, EndTurn are command objects validated and applied to the sim, which emits typed events the scene layer animates. Free wins: undo of uncommitted moves (an AW staple), an AI that issues the exact same commands as the player, and a serializable match log for save/replay.

Key algorithms

  • Movement range — Dijkstra flood-fill from the unit's tile, edge costs from the terrain × movement-class table, budget = min(move points, remaining fuel) since M6. Occupied enemy tiles block; friendly tiles pass-through and are final destinations only for Load and Join.
  • Path arrow — walk the Dijkstra parent tree; if the player traces a custom path with the cursor, keep it while it stays legal and within budget (AW behavior).
  • Attack range — direct units: tiles adjacent to any reachable tile (move-then-fire). Indirect units: ring of min–max range from the current tile only (no move-and-fire).
  • Damage preview — run CombatResolver in dry-run mode; show "Atk 74% / Counter 22%" before the player confirms, exactly like AW.

Start-of-turn sequence (in TurnTransition): +1000 funds per owned property → repair +2 HP for units on friendly properties (paid from funds) → resupply fuel/ammo on properties and next to APCs (M6) → daily fuel burn for air/naval (post-MVP) → all units of the new player become ready.

Combat resolution

damage% = base(atk_unit, def_unit)
  × attacker_hp / 10
  × (1 − 0.1 × terrain_★ × defender_hp / 10)
  + luck(0…9)%

  • HP is 0–100 internally, displayed 1–10 (ceil). Damage% subtracts internal HP directly.
  • base() comes from the DamageChart resource (attacker × defender matrix).
  • Defender counter-attacks with its post-damage HP if it survives, is a direct unit, and the attacker is in its range. Indirect units never counter and are never countered.
  • Luck is a small uniform roll, AW-style; seedable RNG so tests and replays are deterministic.

Capture & economy

  • Properties have 20 capture points. A capturing Infantry/Mech subtracts its displayed HP per turn; moving off or dying resets to 20.
  • Income: 1 000 per owned property at the start of your day.
  • Bases build any unit you can afford; the new unit spawns exhausted (acts next turn).
  • Units on friendly properties repair +2 HP/turn, paid proportionally to unit cost.
  • Victory: capture the enemy HQ, or destroy every enemy unit.

7 · Risks

Scope creep is the killer

Full AW has 26 units, 4 armies, COs, fog, naval. The mitigation is baked into the milestones: the game is fun and complete at M4 with 9 units; everything after is optional depth.

AI quality (M5) is the hardest work

Utility-scored greedy AI is achievable and fun to beat; a genuinely strong AI is a research project. Plan for "credible", not "strong". The sim/presentation split keeps AI iteration cheap.

Asset licensing

No Nintendo assets, names, or trade dress. Use CC0/CC-BY packs or original art (decision below). Keep all third-party licenses in assets/LICENSES.md from day one.

Fog of war retrofit

Fog touches rendering, AI, and rules. Mitigation: from M2 on, all "what can this player see" queries go through one Vision API that simply returns "everything" until M7 implements it.
